## Design Context

### Users
The primary users are church administrators and worship directors preparing weekly worship bulletins. They work under deadline pressure, often reconciling service plans, announcements, volunteers, songs, calendar details, and print layout in one sitting. The job to be done is to turn reliable ministry data into a polished, print-ready bulletin without fighting document layout tools.

### Brand Personality
Calm, efficient, polished, editorial. The interface should feel steady and organized like Planning Center's service plan page, with clear workflow hierarchy, predictable controls, and low visual noise. The Template Designer should feel closer to a Canva-style editor: visual, direct, canvas-centered, and tool-rich without becoming playful or distracting.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-mode-first product UI. Prioritize editorial clarity, structured panels, readable form density, and an understated church-office professionalism. The main editor should resemble Planning Center Online service planning in its calm, task-oriented organization. The Template Designer should support a more visual composition workflow inspired by Canva, with a strong canvas area, clear tool controls, and fast visual feedback.

### Design Principles
- Make weekly production work feel calm and controlled: reduce clutter, preserve hierarchy, and avoid decorative noise.
- Keep dense workflows scannable: labels, groups, and controls should support fast editing under time pressure.
- Treat the bulletin preview and template canvas as the product's center of gravity.
- Prefer polished editorial restraint over trendy effects, generic gradients, or novelty UI.
- Preserve accessible defaults and WCAG AA-level basics even without additional accessibility requirements.
